## 5-Question D-Fillet Output-Identity Preflight Answer Block

### Q1. What output identity does chamfer FilletOp have today?

Chamfer `FilletOp` flows identity through the graph-level resolvers; it has no direct `BRepProvider` / `BRepEdgeProvider` impl. Concretely, in the current code:

- **Graph-level face inheritance (sub-ε.α)** — `OperatorNode::Fillet` arm at `crates/cad-core/src/topology/resolve.rs:234-246` recurses to the unique upstream input and returns the upstream's `Vec<(TopologyFaceId, BRepFaceId)>` unchanged. `FilletOp::evaluate` at `crates/cad-core/src/operators/fillet/mod.rs:367-446` clones upstream `positions` and `indices` verbatim and only APPENDS two chamfer-cap triangles per filleted edge (`fillet/mod.rs:421-432`), so every upstream face still exists bit-identical in the output and identity flows through the recursion.

- **Filtered edge inheritance (sub-ε.β)** — `OperatorNode::Fillet(op)` arm at `crates/cad-core/src/topology/edge_resolve.rs:189-208` recurses, then returns `upstream_edges \ op.edges()` via the `Vec::contains` filter at `edge_resolve.rs:202-207`. Filleted edges are EXCLUDED because their 2-endpoint geometry is absorbed by the chamfer; non-filleted upstream edges retain bit-identical `BRepEdgeId` byte-identity because `BRepEdgeId::for_face_pair` is shape-agnostic and face IDs inherit unchanged.

- **Direct provider non-goals** — explicitly enumerated in the module-doc at `crates/cad-core/src/operators/fillet/mod.rs:30-50`: "No `impl BRepProvider for FilletOp`" and "No `impl BRepEdgeProvider for FilletOp`." The sub-ε.γ inspection halt-and-reframed cap-face provider work because all three candidate paths (cache upstream IDs, mint `KIND_FILLET_CAP` IDs, return empty Vec) tripped halt-fast guardrails; see ADR-119 Context section at `docs/adr/ADR-119-real-round-fillet-substrate.md:37-40`.

- **Chamfer caps in labels/projection** — ADR-120 unparked face-label propagation: `FilletOp::evaluate` preserves upstream `face_labels` when present and pushes `TopologyFaceId::DEGENERATE` for the two appended cap triangles per filleted edge at `fillet/mod.rs:434-437`. `output_is_labeled` mirrors the input at `fillet/mod.rs:453-455`. The DEGENERATE sentinel is defined at `crates/cad-core/src/tessellation/mesh.rs:50-54`. In cad-projection, the lazy resolver `CadProjection::brep_face_id_for_triangle` at `crates/cad-projection/src/lib.rs:185-203` matches a triangle's `TopologyFaceId` against the resolver's mapping; DEGENERATE labels never match a real entry and resolve to `None`. `CadProjection::pick_face` at `crates/cad-projection/src/picking.rs:194-259` consumes the same lookup and treats `None`-resolving triangles as **transparent**, not occluding, per the LOAD-BEARING selection rule in `picking.rs:7-14`. `render_adapter::render_mesh_for` at `crates/cad-projection/src/render_adapter.rs:90-106` passes labels through as opaque `u64`s without re-deriving identity. Stable cap-face IDs are not minted; `docs/architecture/FILLET_OUTPUT_IDENTITY.md:42-55` records this as "Do chamfer caps need stable B-Rep face IDs? That remains deferred until a consumer needs to select, persist, materialize, or otherwise address chamfer-cap faces across rebuilds."

### Q2. What output identity does RoundFilletOp have today?

Real `RoundFilletOp` flows identity through the graph-level resolvers in parallel to chamfer (per ADR-119 D5), with the substantive differences that match ADR D2 (curved-edge inheritance) and D4 (faces retain `BRepFaceId` under face-strip removal). It also has no direct `BRepProvider` / `BRepEdgeProvider` impl. Concretely:

- **Graph-level face resolver behavior (ADR D4)** — `OperatorNode::RoundFillet(_)` arm at `crates/cad-core/src/topology/resolve.rs:248-264` recurses to the unique upstream and returns the upstream's `Vec<(TopologyFaceId, BRepFaceId)>` unchanged. The arm's comment captures the rationale: identity = semantic surface, not mesh shape; faces survive face-strip-removal substitution because the affected face's triangle indices change but the surface identity does not.

- **Graph-level edge resolver behavior (ADR D2)** — `OperatorNode::RoundFillet(_)` arm at `crates/cad-core/src/topology/edge_resolve.rs:210-229` recurses and returns ALL upstream edges unchanged, **including** the filleted ones. This is the substantive divergence from chamfer's filter-the-selection arm: `BRepEdgeId::for_face_pair` is shape-agnostic, so a curved edge between the same two faces retains the same byte-identity. The contract is pinned by `resolver_cuboid_then_round_fillet_inherits_all_edges_including_filleted` at `edge_resolve.rs:717-768`.

- **Inherited faces/edges + nameless cylinder/cap/corner surfaces (ADR D3)** — `RoundFilletOp::evaluate` at `crates/cad-core/src/operators/round_fillet/mod.rs:515-1003` clones upstream positions verbatim, substitutes filleted-edge endpoint indices in the two adjacent faces' triangles with new inset vertices (`mod.rs:1067-1101`, `mod.rs:1103-1151`), and APPENDS new geometry for cylinder surface triangles (`mod.rs:953-970`), Path-branch swept-cylinder ring stitches (`mod.rs:720-738`), and multi-edge corner-patch fan triangles (`mod.rs:1153-1207`). Every appended triangle pushes `TopologyFaceId::DEGENERATE` into `face_labels` (`round_fillet/mod.rs:733-736`, `:967-969`, `:1202-1204`). The cuboid → round-fillet projection smoke at `crates/cad-projection/tests/round_fillet_brep_face_id_lookup_smoke.rs:160-200` pins the contract: DEGENERATE cap/corner triangles resolve to `None`; inherited Cuboid labels resolve to the upstream's stable `BRepFaceId`.

- **Direct provider non-goals** — explicitly enumerated in the module-doc at `crates/cad-core/src/operators/round_fillet/mod.rs:52-76`: "No `impl BRepProvider for RoundFilletOp`" and "No `impl BRepEdgeProvider for RoundFilletOp`" and "No cap-face / corner-patch `BRepFaceId`; caps + corner patches remain nameless in v0 (ADR D3; pressure-deferred), and their triangles emit `TopologyFaceId::DEGENERATE`."

- **`TopologyFaceId::DEGENERATE` usage** — `crates/cad-core/src/tessellation/mesh.rs:50-60` defines the sentinel as `u64::MAX` with `is_degenerate()`. Round-fillet uses it for all three appended geometries (cylinder surface, path-branch ring stitches, corner-patch fan). The picker / render-adapter / face-resolver consumers in cad-projection already treat DEGENERATE as transparent / nameless per Q1.

### Q3. Which remaining gap is actually load-bearing for the next CAD product step?

Each plausible identity gap is classified below. Every classification is grounded in current file/line evidence; none requires speculating about an undocumented consumer.

- **Direct `BRepProvider` impl on `FilletOp` / `RoundFilletOp`** — **already-solved**. The graph-level face resolver at `crates/cad-core/src/topology/resolve.rs:234-264` provides the honest identity surface (inherited upstream IDs); both operators are explicitly identity-preserving and do not mint local face IDs. ADR-119 D5 + D6 + the sub-ε.γ halt-and-reframe (recorded at `docs/adr/ADR-119-real-round-fillet-substrate.md:37-38`) confirm direct provider impls are not the right shape.

- **Direct `BRepEdgeProvider` impl on `FilletOp` / `RoundFilletOp`** — **already-solved**. The edge resolver at `crates/cad-core/src/topology/edge_resolve.rs:189-229` handles both shapes: chamfer's filter-the-selection arm and round's full-inheritance arm. Module-doc non-goals at `fillet/mod.rs:42-50` and `round_fillet/mod.rs:67-71` document this as substrate-honest.

- **Stable `BRepFaceId`s for generated `RoundFilletOp` cylinder / cap / corner-patch surfaces** — **pressure-deferred**. No production consumer in the current codebase addresses these surfaces by ID: `editor/**` contains zero references to `RoundFilletOp` or `round_fillet` (verified by `grep -r RoundFillet editor/` returning no files). The single existing cad-projection consumer test at `crates/cad-projection/tests/round_fillet_brep_face_id_lookup_smoke.rs:160-200` actively asserts `None` for DEGENERATE labels and treats that as the load-bearing contract, not a defect. ADR-119 D3 at `docs/adr/ADR-119-real-round-fillet-substrate.md:90-99` is explicit: "No consumer pressure today; …doctrine-as-substrate posture: inventing identity infrastructure ahead of consumer pressure trips the validation-framework / centralization / taxonomy / extraction pressure anti-pattern."

- **Stable `BRepFaceId`s for chamfer `FilletOp` cap geometry** — **pressure-deferred**. Same posture as the round case; `docs/architecture/FILLET_OUTPUT_IDENTITY.md:42-55` (the controlling doctrine doc) explicitly states the question is deferred "until a consumer needs to select, persist, materialize, or otherwise address chamfer-cap faces across rebuilds." No such consumer exists today; the projection smoke contract is `None` for DEGENERATE caps (`brep_face_id_for_triangle` semantics at `crates/cad-projection/src/lib.rs:155-203`).

- **Face-label propagation for chamfer `FilletOp`** — **already-solved**. ADR-120 unparked the labeled-output case; `FilletOp::evaluate` at `crates/cad-core/src/operators/fillet/mod.rs:434-437` preserves upstream labels and emits `TopologyFaceId::DEGENERATE` for cap triangles. `output_is_labeled` mirrors the input flag at `fillet/mod.rs:453-455`. `FILLET_OUTPUT_IDENTITY.md` records this as PARTIALLY RESOLVED.

- **Face-label propagation for `RoundFilletOp`** — **already-solved**. `RoundFilletOp::evaluate` at `crates/cad-core/src/operators/round_fillet/mod.rs:986-991` constructs the output `Tessellation` with labels; the face-strip-removal substitution preserves the substituted triangles' upstream labels (`mod.rs:1067-1101`) while all appended geometries (cylinder, path-branch, corner-fan) emit `TopologyFaceId::DEGENERATE` (`mod.rs:733-736`, `:967-969`, `:1202-1204`).

- **Edge inheritance behavior — chamfer (filtered) vs round (full)** — **already-solved**. The two resolver arms encode the contract clearly and are pinned by unit tests at `crates/cad-core/src/topology/edge_resolve.rs:519-616` (chamfer: filter selected, retain rest byte-equal) and `edge_resolve.rs:717-822` (round: retain ALL including filleted, byte-equal). ADR-119 D2 rationale at `docs/adr/ADR-119-real-round-fillet-substrate.md:79-89` explains the semantic divergence.

- **Picking / selection** — **already-solved**. `CadProjection::pick_face` at `crates/cad-projection/src/picking.rs:194-259` returns the closest *resolvable* face hit, treating DEGENERATE / unowned / topology-changing / out-of-bounds triangles as transparent. The module-level selection rule at `picking.rs:5-42` ties this directly to ADR-120 label propagation and ADR-119's nameless cap geometry. The round-fillet-specific smoke `pick_resolves_round_filleted_geometry_top_face` at `crates/cad-projection/tests/face_picking_smoke.rs:544-625` proves transparency end-to-end through a `RoundFilletOp` root.

- **Projection / highlight support** — **already-solved** for inherited faces; **pressure-deferred** for cap/corner highlight. `CadProjection::face_triangle_indices` at `crates/cad-projection/src/lib.rs:238-271` already produces dense triangle indices for highlight overlays against any resolvable face. Cap-face-specific highlight would require minting cap-face IDs (the same pressure-deferred work as above) and so reduces to the cap-ID question, not a separate gap.

No plausible gap is **`consumer-pressure-present`** in the current code; **none** is **`needs-ADR`** because ADR-119 (substrate + D3 nameless-in-v0 policy) and ADR-120 (chamfer face-label propagation) already pin the policy and explicitly leave the lift path open for when concrete pressure surfaces.
