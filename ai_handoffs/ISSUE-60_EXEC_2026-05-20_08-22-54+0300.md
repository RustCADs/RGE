  builds a real `MaterialGraph` with three material nodes (`albedo`,
  `normal`, `output`) and two typed-port `MaterialEdge` connections
  (`Color->Color`, `Vector->Texture`), feeds the graph to
  `NodeGraphWidget::new().model_from(&graph)`, and asserts the resulting
  model:
  - `node_count() == 3`, `edge_count() == 2`;
  - every node `display_name` equals its material key (`"albedo"`,
    `"normal"`, `"output"`);
  - every node `kind` equals the adapter's static `"MaterialNode"` string;
  - the edge whose `EdgeId` was returned by `connect(albedo, output, ...)`
    has `src == albedo`, `dst == output`, `label == "color->color"`;
  - the edge whose `EdgeId` was returned by `connect(normal, output, ...)`
    has `src == normal`, `dst == output`, `label == "vector->texture"`.
  Records are looked up by the stable `NodeId` / `EdgeId` returned from
  graph construction, so the assertions do not depend on insertion order or
  on the substrate's `BTreeMap`-sorted iteration order.
